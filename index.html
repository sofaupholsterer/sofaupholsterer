Sofa Upholstery: Restore Comfort and Style

Refreshing your sofa with professional upholstery is one of the smartest ways to extend the life of a favorite piece while updating the room’s style. Whether your sofa shows signs of wear, the fabric no longer matches your décor, or the cushioning has lost its bounce, a careful reupholstery job can transform the piece and save you the expense and waste of replacement.

When to Reupholster vs Replace

Before committing to a new sofa, evaluate the frame, springs, and overall construction. If the frame is solid and the springs or webbing can be repaired, reupholstery is usually worth it. Replace when the structure is damaged beyond reasonable repair or when the cost of reupholstering approaches the price of a new, comparable sofa.

Benefits of Professional Upholstery

  • Cost-effectiveness: Reupholstering often costs less than a high-quality replacement and preserves the value of well-made frames.
  • Customization: Choose any fabric, pattern, or trim to match your interior design goals.
  • Sustainability: Reuse existing furniture and reduce landfill waste.
  • Improved Comfort: Replace worn foam and springs for better support and ergonomics.

How the Process Works

Professional upholstery typically follows these steps: inspection, disassembly, structural repairs (if needed), replacement of padding and springs, pattern cutting, sewing, and final fitting. Experienced upholsterers retain original pieces as templates, ensuring new fabric aligns with the sofa’s contours and original design lines.

Choosing Fabric and Filling

Fabric selection depends on use, aesthetic, and maintenance needs. Common choices include:

  • Natural fibers (linen, cotton): breathable and comfortable but may require protective treatments.
  • Wool blends: durable and resistant to pilling with a luxurious feel.
  • Performance fabrics: engineered for stain resistance and easy cleaning—ideal for homes with children or pets.
  • Leather: durable and ages well but needs proper conditioning.

For filling, high-resilience foam with a dacron wrap is a common choice for modern sofas, providing a balance between support and cushioning. Down or feather fills offer plush comfort but require regular maintenance to avoid flattening.

Cost Considerations

Costs vary widely based on fabric choice, complexity of the sofa, and labor. Simple two-seater sofas with standard fabric will cost less than custom fabrics, intricate piping, or sofas with detailed trims and tufting. Always request a clear written estimate that breaks down materials, labor, and any repairs.

Finding the Right Upholsterer
